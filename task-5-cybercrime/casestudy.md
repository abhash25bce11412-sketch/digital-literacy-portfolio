# Task 5: Cybercrime Case Study
**Topic:** UPI "Request Money" Fraud (Online Payment Fraud)

### Description
UPI fraud is a type of financial cybercrime where attackers exploit the "Request Money" feature of UPI apps (like GPay, PhonePe, or Paytm) to trick victims into authorizing a debit from their own account instead of receiving a payment.

### How it Happens (Step-by-Step)
1. **The Hook:** The attacker finds a victim on a second-hand selling platform (like OLX) or social media. They express urgent interest in buying an item.
2. **The Deception:** The attacker claims they are sending a "payment link" or "advance deposit" to the victim.
3. **The Trap:** Instead of sending money, the attacker sends a "Collect Request" via a UPI app. They often tell the victim, "Just enter your PIN to receive the money."
4. **The Theft:** The victim, often in a hurry or unaware of how UPI works, enters their UPI PIN. This authorizes the bank to *deduct* money from the victim's account and send it to the attacker.

### Target Audience
College students and young professionals who frequently use digital payments for quick transactions or online selling are the primary targets due to their high volume of UPI usage.

### Consequences
The immediate consequence is the loss of hard-earned money, which is often difficult to recover once the transaction is authorized. Secondary consequences include emotional distress and a loss of trust in digital financial systems.
