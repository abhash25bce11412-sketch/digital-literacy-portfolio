# 'Stay Safe Online' Checklist for Students
*Follow these tips to protect your digital identity and finances.*

1. **The PIN Rule:** Never enter your UPI PIN to *receive* money. A PIN is only required to *send* or deduct money from your account.
2. **Verify the Source:** Before clicking any link in an SMS or WhatsApp message, verify the sender’s identity. Banks never ask for sensitive info via text.
3. **Two-Factor Authentication (2FA):** Enable 2FA on your Gmail, GitHub, and social media accounts to prevent unauthorized access.
4. **Public Wi-Fi Warning:** Avoid logging into bank accounts or making UPI payments while connected to free public Wi-Fi (like at cafes or stations).
5. **Check the 'Collect' Request:** Always read the pop-up notification in your UPI app carefully. If it says "Request by..." or "Pay...", decline it immediately.
6. **Unique Passwords:** Use a password manager to ensure you aren't reusing the same password for your college portal and your bank.
7. **App Permissions:** Regularly audit your mobile apps and revoke permissions (camera, microphone, contacts) for apps that don't need them.
8. **Privacy Settings:** Keep your social media profiles private to prevent identity thieves from gathering personal data for social engineering.

### 🚨 How to Report a Cybercrime in India
If you have been cheated or notice suspicious activity:
* **National Helpline:** Call **1930** immediately (The Golden Hour is critical for freezing funds).
* **Official Portal:** Report the incident at [cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in).
